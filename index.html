B-SKILL: Contact-Aware Bimanual Skill Learning for Generalizable Humanoid Manipulation

***
***
***
***

Abstract

Exerting precise and controllable contacts on objects to achieve desired task goals is a fundamental yet highly challenging capability in robotic dexterous manipulation, particularly in contact-rich bimanual scenarios. To address this challenge, we propose B-SKILL, a multi-stage bimanual skill learning framework designed to capture the essential structure of hand–object interactions across tasks and enable efficient generalization and stable execution of human bimanual skills on diverse dexterous robotic hands. First, B-SKILL learns a hand trajectory imitation policy from human motion capture demonstrations, accurately reproducing the spatio-temporal coordination patterns of hand movements. Building on this foundation, we introduce explicit hand–object contact modeling and perform residual refinement of the interaction policy, allowing the policy to actively optimize contact behaviors and execution robustness while preserving the original trajectory intent. We conduct extensive evaluations of B-SKILL in simulation across 17 bimanual manipulation tasks and three types of dexterous hands. The results show that our framework outperforms state-of-the-art methods in terms of task success rate, contact stability, and cross-task generalization. Furthermore, we present real-world demonstrations of a humanoid robot performing complex chemical experiments, including the iodine clock reaction, the purple cabbage color-change reaction, and fluorescence experiments, showcasing the practical feasibility of the learned behaviors.
